# Mario Forever: Advanced Mario Bros.

**Mario Forever: Advanced Mario Bros.** is a fan game developed by **AppliconReal** with **Cloud Engine 2.2** which takes place four years after the events of **Mario Forever**. It aims to feel close to the original game while providing new challenges along with a challenging final boss fight. A few people may recognize it from its Mario Worker Remake prototype.

**Developing & Level Designing:** AppliconReal

**Plot:** Four years after Mario's victory against Bowser, Toadstool is yet again invaded by Bowser. Princess Peach has been kidnapped, and the entirety of Toadstool is bombarded with chaos and disaster. However, without a second thought, Mario volunteers to defeat Bowser in battle to save Toadstool from the dark age once again. His brother, Luigi, too; couldn't just sit back and watch. And so, he decides to make his own ways in defeating Bowser, agreeing to meet Mario some time later so that they can pursue their heroic goals together.

**Number of Levels:** 30

**Themes**:

-   World 1: Overworld / Waterland
-   World 2: Starland
-   World 3: Skyland
-   World 4: Fastrun / Afternoon
-   World 5: Pipeland
-   World 6: Darkland

**Difficulty:** Hard

## Features

-   **Continue System:** Players have the option to choose between playing with or without continues. This is togglable in the settings and is on by default.
-   **Remixed Soundtracks:** Some level soundtracks have their separate covers/remixes or an alternative. You can choose to listen to them or toggle it off in the settings to listen to the original songs. In the future, official music packs may also be provided.
-   **Freestyle:** You can select any level you'd like to play, even if you haven't completed Story Mode. Stuck in a level for too long? You have the option to skip that level. "Single Level Mode" allows you to play only a specific level without having to go through the rest of the world.
-   **Buffed Powerups:** Beetroots are far more convenient here in comparison to the original game. You can shoot up to four beetroots (compared to the usual two), and they can kill some indestructible enemies. Boomerangs are present, and you can shoot up to four of them as well. They can kill nearly all indestructible enemies.

### Changes

-   Redesigned the logo.
-   Changed the Main Menu music.
-   Given that continues are on, your progress is now saved level-wise instead of world-wise.
-   Changed the font for HUDs to Junebug.
-   Level selection is now possible in Freestyle Mode. For convenience, you should be able to access it even before completing Story Mode.
-   Changed the number of continues (4 → ∞).
-   Changed the soundtrack for AMB 4's world map since it is already used in one of the world's levels.
-   Visual improvements to World 2-2, 3-2, and 4-1.
-   Visual improvement to the introduction.
-   Clouds now emit a faint glow if effects are turned on.
-   Better sound adjustment to AMB 1's map music and World 3's finale.
-   Improved the quality of world maps.
-   Level timers are now time-based instead of frame-based.
-   **Added:** Levels 16 (4-2) to 30 (6-6 / 6-A).
-   **Added:** New remixes/alternative soundtracks have been added to the existing levels.
-   **Added:** An extra option in the settings which allows you to customize the level HUD. You can choose between having it show the level titles (e.g. `Starlit Platforms`) or the level formatting (e.g. `4-1`).
-   **Added:** A parallax effect for underground, underwater, and fortress levels. Due to one of the many limitations of CTF 2.5+, this feature is not yet togglable. Workarounds may however be done in the future.
-   **Added:** Patterning coins in some levels which indicate that there is an invisible power-up block in that spot.
-   **Added:** New hidden/invisible one-up blocks in some levels.
-   **Added:** A functionality which allows you to scroll faster through maps by holding the `Shift` key.
-   **Added:** A new method to trigger the secret 1-UP trick in the Save Game Room.
-   **Removed:** A falling block in the finale of the first game because it was too difficult to dodge.

### Patches

-   Removed an invisible barrier which stops Luigi from entering a specific pipe in 4-1. This invisible "barrier" was as a result of a now-scrapped mechanic.
-   Green Bullet Bill Launchers cannot shoot to the right. This has been fixed by correcting the animation's hot spots, which was what forced us to disable a few lines of events.
-   Fixed a bug where overlapping the platform towards the end of 1-2 causes it to fall even if you haven't landed on it.
-   Fixed a bug where smooth scrolling occasionally bugs out the bossfight in AMB 3.
-   Fixed a bug where falling Koopa Shells are gold in levels where Gold Paratroopas are present, even if the base Koopa Troopa isn't gold.
-   Fixed a rare bug where the camera may slightly shake upon going left during a boss fight.
